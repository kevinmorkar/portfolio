🔬 Research Overview
  • Conducted advanced Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS) analysis on photovoltaic solar cells to characterize internal resistance, charge transfer, and diffusion processes
  • Developed equivalent circuit models (Randles circuit) to simulate and interpret impedance behaviour across varying frequency ranges (mHz to MHz)
  • Investigated the effect of temperature, illumination intensity, and degradation on solar cell impedance parameters to assess long-term performance
  • Performed data acquisition using precision impedance analysers and processed results using MATLAB and ZView software for Nyquist & Bode plot analysis
📈 Key Findings & Impact
  • Identified key degradation mechanisms in crystalline silicon PV cells through impedance shift patterns, contributing to reliability prediction models
  • Quantified charge transfer resistance and double-layer capacitance providing insight into recombination losses that reduce photovoltaic efficiency
  • Findings directly applicable to next-generation solar cell research including perovskite and thin-film technologies
  • Research methodology aligns with PhD-level investigation into smart energy harvesting, grid-connected PV diagnostics and energy storage systems
  • Produced detailed technical thesis covering experimental methodology, circuit modelling, statistical analysis and engineering conclusions
